Getting Started with FinTrack

Welcome to FinTrack, your personal finance companion! This guide will help you understand how to use our tools to manage your finances effectively.

What is FinTrack?

FinTrack is a comprehensive personal finance tool that helps you track your income, expenses, debt, and investments. Our goal is to provide you with the tools you need to take control of your financial future.

Key Features

  • Budget Planner

    Track your income and expenses to create a monthly budget.

  • Debt Calculator

    Plan your debt repayment strategy using avalanche or snowball methods.

  • Investment Calculator

    Project your investment growth over time with compound interest.

  • Privacy-Focused

    All your data is stored locally on your device, not on our servers.

Navigation Guide

FinTrack is designed to be intuitive and easy to navigate. Here's a quick overview of the main sections:

  • Home: Overview of all features and quick budget calculator
  • Budgeting: Detailed budget planner with income and expense tracking
  • Debt Management: Tools for planning debt repayment strategies
  • Investment: Calculator for projecting investment growth
  • Help: Comprehensive guide and FAQs (you are here)

Budget Planner Guide

The Budget Planner helps you track your income and expenses to create a balanced monthly budget.

How to Use the Budget Planner

1

Add Income Sources

Click on the "Income" tab and add all your income sources (salary, freelance work, etc.).

2

Add Expenses

Switch to the "Expenses" tab and add your monthly expenses by category (housing, food, transportation, etc.).

3

Review Budget Summary

The Budget Summary section will automatically calculate your total income, expenses, and balance.

4

Save Your Budget

Click the "Save Budget" button to store your budget data locally on your device.

Understanding the Budget Chart

The pie chart in the Budget Summary shows the breakdown of your expenses by category. This visual representation helps you identify where your money is going and which areas you might need to adjust.

Pro Tip

Aim to keep your essential expenses (housing, food, utilities) under 50% of your income for a balanced budget.

Debt Calculator Guide

The Debt Calculator helps you plan your debt repayment strategy to become debt-free faster.

How to Use the Debt Calculator

1

Add Your Debts

Enter the details of each debt including name, current balance, interest rate, and minimum payment.

2

Choose a Repayment Strategy

Select either the Avalanche method (highest interest first) or Snowball method (smallest balance first).

3

Add Extra Payment (Optional)

If you can afford to pay more than the minimum payments, enter an additional amount to accelerate your debt payoff.

4

Calculate Payoff

Click "Calculate Payoff" to see how long it will take to become debt-free and how much interest you'll pay.

Avalanche vs. Snowball Method

Avalanche Method

How it works: Pay minimum on all debts, then put extra money toward the debt with the highest interest rate.

Best for: Minimizing the total interest paid and becoming debt-free faster mathematically.

Advantage: Saves the most money in interest over time.

Snowball Method

How it works: Pay minimum on all debts, then put extra money toward the debt with the smallest balance.

Best for: Building momentum through quick wins as you eliminate smaller debts first.

Advantage: Provides psychological motivation as you see debts being paid off faster.

Investment Calculator Guide

The Investment Calculator helps you project how your investments might grow over time with compound interest.

How to Use the Investment Calculator

1

Enter Initial Investment

Enter the amount you're starting with (can be zero if you're starting from scratch).

2

Set Monthly Contribution

Enter how much you plan to add to your investment each month.

3

Specify Expected Return

Enter your expected annual return rate (%). You can use the slider to adjust this value.

4

Set Investment Period

Enter how many years you plan to invest for.

5

Choose Compound Frequency

Select how often your investment compounds (monthly, quarterly, or annually).

6

Calculate Growth

Click "Calculate" to see how your investment might grow over time.

Understanding the Results

The investment calculator provides several key pieces of information:

  • Final Balance: The total value of your investment at the end of the period
  • Total Contributions: How much money you put in (initial + monthly contributions)
  • Interest Earned: How much your money grew through compound interest
  • Year-by-Year Breakdown: A table showing how your investment grows each year

Pro Tip

The power of compound interest works best over long periods. Even small increases in your investment period can lead to significant growth in your final balance.

Data Storage

FinTrack is designed with your privacy in mind. Here's how we handle your data:

Local Storage

All your financial data is stored locally on your device using your browser's localStorage feature. This means:

  • Your data never leaves your device
  • We don't have access to your financial information
  • Your data persists between sessions on the same device
  • Clearing your browser data will also clear your FinTrack data

Backing Up Your Data

Since your data is stored locally, it's important to know how to back it up:

1

Save Your Budget/Plan

Use the "Save" button in each tool to ensure your data is stored in localStorage.

2

Export Feature (Coming Soon)

In a future update, you'll be able to export your data as CSV or PDF files for backup purposes.

Privacy & Security

We take your privacy and security seriously. Here's our approach:

Privacy Policy

FinTrack is designed to be privacy-focused:

  • We don't collect any personal or financial data
  • We don't use cookies to track you
  • We don't have user accounts or logins
  • All calculations happen on your device, not our servers

Security Considerations

While FinTrack is secure by design, here are some additional security tips:

  • Don't use FinTrack on public or shared computers if you're concerned about others seeing your financial data
  • Consider clearing your browser data if you've used FinTrack on a shared device
  • Remember that clearing browser data will erase your saved financial information

Frequently Asked Questions

Is FinTrack completely free?

Yes, FinTrack is completely free to use. There are no hidden fees, subscriptions, or premium features.

Will I lose my data if I clear my browser history?

Yes, if you clear your browser data (specifically localStorage), you will lose your saved financial information. We recommend exporting your data regularly once that feature becomes available.

Can I access my FinTrack data on multiple devices?

Currently, your data is stored locally on each device. This means data on one device won't automatically sync to another device. We're planning to add data export/import features in the future to make this easier.

How accurate are the calculators?

Our calculators use standard financial formulas and provide reasonably accurate projections. However, real-world results may vary due to factors like changing interest rates, market fluctuations, and other variables. The calculators are best used as planning tools rather than precise predictions.

Are there any limits to how much data I can store?

The amount of data you can store is limited by your browser's localStorage capacity, which is typically around 5-10MB. This is more than enough for most users' financial data.

Troubleshooting

Encountering issues? Here are solutions to common problems:

Common Issues and Solutions

Data Not Saving

If your data isn't being saved between sessions:

  • Make sure you're using the same browser and device
  • Check if your browser has localStorage enabled
  • Ensure you're not in private/incognito browsing mode
  • Click the "Save" button explicitly after making changes

Calculations Seem Incorrect

If calculator results don't seem right:

  • Double-check all input values
  • Ensure you're using the correct units (e.g., percentages as numbers)
  • Try clearing and re-entering your data
  • Refresh the page and try again

Charts Not Displaying

If charts aren't showing up:

  • Make sure you have JavaScript enabled in your browser
  • Try using a different browser
  • Ensure you have entered enough data to generate a chart
  • Refresh the page and try again
